Biography

Life is an Exploration, Art is a Journey, and no one lives these principles to the fullest than Jerry Verschoor

‘He Thumbs through the pages of his life, his soul bites out with ancestry force and his life surrounds his every move as his child constantly guiding him from over his shoulder.’

A man with no single aim or direction; A man with too many fingers and not enough toes; Or as he puts it, a man with too many fingers in too many pies and not enough toes to stand in any direction. A creative gypsy of the world, A gypsy among his fellow travellers on the roads of change and what may come. A traveller of life and wonderous worlds of nature and the imagination. Living with no fixed abode and exploring with the tide of the unexpected. playing with art in many of its imaginative variety, dabbles the many creative disciplines with his wild imaginative explorations, as long as it is creative and fun. no one style or one topic, even one avenue in the art world, just let’s see what happens, enjoying the journey of what comes out of it, see what can be mixed and used to bring the ideas to life. He is a narrative in his own right, an ever unfolding, living and breathing, continuation of Artistic Chemistry.

"I'm not Unique, in the real sense of the word, but guided by the Energy and Wisdom of Nature."

Not unique, just an individual living in constant meditation of travels to imaginary worlds and concepts, never seeing the world or objects as they are but the many varieties of what they could be, mixing totally different elements, objects, ideas to create a surprised one, into an altogether imaginative landscape of Dreams versus Expectations, always having very itchy feet in both reality and fantasy/imagination. not afraid to lose everything but his own creative journeys, is the core that drives his life.

"The importance, to me, is to see this reaction, feel the experience, the process of what comes out, - to see what can be mixed, and how to make it speak in a new and different language by bringing out the narrative, making it a Statement. "

Restarted from nothing, reshaping his life many times, shaking normality upside down is the adventure of this Peter Pan in the 21 Century. Exploring many social circles and lives, connecting with spiritual ideas and guidelines joined with natures energy and wisdom since childhood, this dragon has never grown up and wishes never to be societies adult. age is just a number for his soul is 12 years old with an 18-year-olds desire and passion and an 80-year-old wisdom and experience all guided by a 500-year-old spirit. Experimenting to extremities in any area of human life and creative cosmoses, pushes boundaries any way he can where no limit can be seen in his eyes. this perfectionist and a detail freak breathe with one question alone, that human question on the death bed, “did I live my life?”. he walks with eyes on two questions staring at the faces of everything, ‘how and why’. how does it work and how can I mix it in my creation? why does it work, why is it there and why is it that way? two questions also aimed inwards to his feelings and experiences.

‘life is easy, just society makes it complex’

A pure, humble, caring, honest and emotional man who shares with others his adventures and discoveries of being real, cares for himself less than those he loves dearly “as the kindness needs to be returned twice fold”. He is very particular with the choices he makes but often blindly jumps in without thinking and makes it work, his brother often makes the comment of:

“no matter how many times he falls he still stands on his feet”.

An introvert by nature who has learned to be an extravert with belief and creativity sharing with a witty and sarcastic sense of humour, usually laughing at himself. Having a high respect for women and less for the ‘man man’, for he himself is in spirit and soul both male and female.

“He who wishes too hard falls too hard for the pictures than the reality and thus feels higher with his illusion than the truths of the factual”

A creative and artist who has mainly work as a designer for films, designing many elements within the film production, also designing interiors, costumes, furniture, etc. His personal work is poetry, drawing and sculptural work, as well as redesigning and recycling old objects into unique sculptural and functional pieces. his interests and focus in creativity are nature, culture and the arts. he loves playing with ideas and materials regardless what it is and just lets his imagination travel, which is often very different than what anyone ever imagined. Never ask jerry to go crazy on the imagination, a request often asked and a request often regretted by clients for the trip took them far beyond their experience.

“Art is a lifestyle and a state of mind outside the social structures and is never a job, it is purity of mind, body and soul and the natural journey of exploration with uncontrolled imagination, do not be scared to let it go and see what happens. This is the way of being who we are.”

"My Childhood was a Dreamscape shaped by Mother Nature's touching hands. I grew up surrounded by all of her Elements, the Bushland, the Sky and the Sea, in an endlessness of freedom. Like the Nankeen Kestrel glidingly navigating the unlimited. I was an Introvert. Through my Art I have learned to be an Extrovert, expressing my constant meditations of travels."

As an isolated shy child in a family of four kids, two mothers and a troubled father, who is now calm and joyful with himself, struggled hard to connect with family and social situations, spending most of his childhood travelling his own worlds in his room, in the garden, and very often oneness with nature. the escape travelled this alone and lost child into the wilderness nearby where the wings of the guiding mother nature comforted him to peace and safety. These regular unities with nature, observing and respecting the truth of her power, balance and love has never left his life. With inner peace and protected from danger she guided him back to exactly the same place he entered the wilderness.

“Australia is a harsh land and easily to get lost and die in, but with purity, trust and untainted respect this land will protect and guide you.”

The teenager continued explorations and bonding in the vastness of nature, bushwalks in the sounds of mother, sleeping with the stars, enveloping rocks and lakes between the toes and mostly with just a torch in hand, relaxing with the dark echoing silence of unexplored caves.

His dad has mentioned that:

“As a baby of less than a year he often disappeared with his mother asking where is Jerry, I usually find him in his room playing, when I opened the door, there was Jerry on the floor adding a piece to a perfectly balanced sculpture he has just built, much taller than he was, he turned and smiled at me, then knocked it all down with a giggle and started all over again. This was normal in the Jerry’s world.”

His father often answers the question of why did Jerry become an artist, a word Jerry doesn’t like to call himself, creative is the word he uses, “Jerry never became a creative, he was born that way.” With one parent a painter and artist and the other a craftswoman, adding to him failing every subject in school except the Art of which he scored very high and winning every art contest he entered as a child, no wonder his father makes that statement.

Many of his childhood friends have said that: “Jerry was never really in this world, always dreaming and imagining other places we can never understand. But it was a lot of fun him trying to bring us into those worlds”

Failed every subject in school except Art, his grades dropped well short of university entrance, yet he has a BA degree from one of the top universities in Perth Western Australia. Senior heads often journied to high schools for new recruits and when they stumbled across Jerry, they were blown away with his exhibition. Most students have assignment drawings and paintings, but Jerry had a space four time larger, full of originally imagined paintings, drawings, sculptures, miniatures and poetry. “you will be in regardless of your grades” remarked the head of the Art department.

“Jerry would have made it with or without the university, as he was already that good and creative and in his own world.”

Poetry was a desperate natural need to find the answers to a confusing world that ousted him. Joining Christian groups in his early teens he soon discovered the answers was not available and was strongly suggested to leave. Knocking on doors one, two and three within the confinements of social conditioning the journey drove him to only one conclusive option, “all we seek with truth in hand is all we are in ourselves and natures understand.” Searching in poetry opened the doors society slammed, an angry shy introvert locked the poems in the rubbish bin after his discovery and frustrations were spilled out onto the page. never seeing the light of day, believing no one would care for them. A high school stage performance with his close friend, rewriting famous songs with their own lyrics, the school exhibition and a Punk band he was in in university was the only public taste of his earlier poetic works. In his early thirties, his closest friend and first true love and muse, Marianne accidently discovered his poem amongst some drawings he gave her. Asking if it was his, and with a ‘OH SHIT’ shy yes adding of their travels to the rubbish bin urged the shocked, begging request to thrown them to her. This amazing acceptance, sharing his emotions, with deep discussions about them created an outburst of often 5 poems a day drifting on her desk. years of boxes and boxes of original poetry stacked up in her mother’s house, sadly a fire destroyed the whole house. This collaboration of sharing and finding the answers still continue to this day.

The practice of writing to his confidant continued well after he left London to work in a studio in the US. She stayed in London receiving many poems via email and post. A habit that was established through these correspondences, the inspiration for the poem from a discussion with his friends, would receive the original as a thank you, and with much surprise the gift was very well appreciated, one case which affected and encouraged Jerry deeply was his friend asked if the poem could be passed to a friend in need, ‘troubled soul’ was its name. Two weeks after his friend stated that she was about to commit suicide but after reading the poem she changed her mind.

This revelation conquered his fear and doubt and continued to share his poetry with much surprising appreciation and feedback. A close friend volunteered for an organisation called ‘children of the night’ and a willing Jerry wanted to help, he wrote three poems for them, one called ‘Children of the night’. After reading the poem, the children replied with one unanimous question, ‘Is he one of us?’

Completely shattered to tears with this response to his pure and honest way of writing, Jerry continued with more passion and trust. The original purpose of answering his own questions did not change, but his answers helping others did. Working on designing costumes for a surreal musical his friend was writing, a story of Noah’s Arc with the underlining theme of the Holocaust, a theme strongly related to Jerry. A university photographic assignment opened doors into the secret life of a Jewish family, once presented with an elegantly presented personally made book the father brought Jerry to tears with, “Thank your family for saving our race”, being a dutchman opened so many doors of respect with historical kindness echoing throughout the generations. ‘Noah’s Arc’ was no different and his emotions with nature, Jerry added new layers to the costume designs of the musical, A poem also came from his tearful research upon the discovery of a photo in Life magazine. A reading to the production comity with the investors being survivors, crying and requesting the poem for the Holocaust museum in LA. The poem is called ‘Ragged dolled starving human beings’

A growth and journey of surprising unexpected twists developed into the body of poetry works he has today. Even though hundreds were lost with the fire at his friend’s mothers house in England, flooding at his other friend’s house in Spain, termite attach at his father’s house in Australia and being thrown away by Jerry and some others who didn’t appreciate his work, the collection is still large, strong and continuing. Sculptural and design works have been created most his life as mentioned earlier, from childhood to now he has been creating original imagination trips for fun, most have been destroyed by time or given away to friends with few photos still existing, but passion has never died with much encouragement from his parents.

Born at home above a toy shop and convenience store owned by his father in the Nederland’s, premature, nearly dying just after birth, and the toy shop section situated under where he was born, developing in a creative environment of craft works in clothing, puppets, jewellery, paintings etc, it’s only natural Jerry lost his reality. 6 years of age and arriving in Australia, not speaking the language, not fitting in with other kids, being bullied throughout schooling years, he naturally escaped into his imagination, creative handicraft works, designing and building his own bedroom at the age of 12, deigning and building the home letterbox at the age of 8, participating in the family touring puppet show throughout Perth, etc.

Star wars phenomime just hit the world and both he and his brother did build models kits from star wars and other sci-fi movies which was displayed at a sci-fi marathon screenings in a theatre, but both continued with designing and building their own models and the reality of those ships, adding them to a huge mural drawing they both drew of all the spaceships designed, all to scale. This scale drawing beginnings continued with his brother drawing and compiling in a book of every car Citroen ever designed and made, all extremely accurate and all to scale, one of many of jerry’s projects follows similar ideas, just jerry being jerry made it an extremely huge project of time, an extremely detailed timeline on a net platform that allows every visual detail to be seen and researched from anywhere in the world at any time in history, a real form of time traveling. The project is too big for jerry to do alone. But history and sculptural works have always played a part in Jerry’s creative outlet, from baby to well in his teens Lego expressed his creative outlet, inventing original pieces for the explorative enjoyment. Every Christmas a new Christmas Lego model would rest next to the tree.

Rubbish and found objects, nature pieces and second hand toys etc travelling his imagination into many fantastical elaborate innovations that most people could not keep up. Early twenties developed more opportunity when destroying old houses became a part time job, a great supply of unique rubbish pieces with a makeover drew interest from friends who wished to own them. This practice still continues in Jerrys creative space. Now joined with his creative partner LiZi, a performance fine-artist and extremely creative in her own right, the two amazing creative home and studio space has a room full of old broken pieces of others rubbish, natures wonder and other materials to use in their creations. The house is adorned with second hand items that was either donated or found in the rubbish adding to the depth of their sense of creativity and awareness of the beauty in other people’s perception of rubbish, plastics and non-natural materials is not welcome.

Drawings and Illustration was nightmares in his reality, the visions were magical, yet struggled at every line placed on the page. Always sketched since very early in his years, but never focused on practical foundations, his father loved his drawings, was always amazed at his skill, but was very critical of jerry’s habit on lined paper, saying with frustrated tones, “your drawings are great but not on lined paper”, a running joke in the relationship of father and son in later years. Father being a painter and illustrator jerry got pushed into many areas of drawing art, painting lessons in nature, being taught how to see and techniques on how to paint and draw, experimenting with many forms of drawing and painting styles and methods, these were tough and fun times for jerry as he just wanted to play with the creativity of paint.

“Drawing was always very hard for me, struggling with every stroke of the pencil or pen. ironic that most of my career have been drawing in the animation and film industry, I always laugh at how I survived for so long.”

In the studios he worked from his mid to late twenties he was known as ‘the one who couldn’t draw but could do amazing life drawings and animation drawings’. An amazing observer of details was he, but construction was very hard. With endless persistence mixed with sleepless nights practicing he conquered his fear of drawing and now does amazing illustrations of both imagination and feeling. Observations of his journey in creativity, the torture and glory are engraved within every page, clearly visible in letter and line. With not fitting into society well, being bullied in all his schooling years, extremely shy to a point that in university he would make and handout a detailed, fully illustrated and photographed book instead of standing in front talking about his research, his metaphorically and compositionally playing creative illustrations and poetry expresses the meaning of all he thinks and feels clearly. These illustrations and poetry all delve into multi-layered messages and meanings in every image and word used.

“I like to paint pictures with my words that touches the imagination and emotions of the reader, the illustrations add to the layers with socially recognised metaphoric icons that have their own cultural meaning, this is the fun part of expressing my honest thoughts and feelings. What’s the point in making a work if others cannot feel it?”

When you see the works, you are always surprised to discover and feel something new. Jerry has overcome his fear of public speaking and has given many lectures on creativity, animation, visual communication etc in universities and conferences all over the world with crowds of thousands at times. Still quiet and shy, an introvert in his isolated space exploring openly the extravert experiences to a point that many people can’t believe jerry is an introvert.

Peter Pan of the 21 Century continues to explore and observe the world and himself to his dying breath, retirement is not a thought in his mind.

“Why should one stop growing and pursuing his dreams till his dying day? Why should one stop living at a certain age that society has forced? Why should one stop being a child in spirit and soul because society says the numbers are too high? Sorry society, I can’t live that way.”

After so many years walking the path of this road on his own, with the occasional partner periodically joining his steps along the way, he has finally found his true soul partner and creative twin. A union that has been together for many lives, and again in this one. With this union of souls, the journey becomes complete for jerry, and the road he has been searching for is now a road he can dance on. The power of this creative union has woken the dragon and sparked new life in his creative universe.

Together they have many creative plans and lifestyle directions that will open the future in an explosion of wonders in nature, spirits and imagination. With full trust and inspiration to the each other as well as perfectionistic opinions towards each other’s works the growth of jerry and LiZi, his creative partner is going to explode in the art world. Both are very talented and skilled in their individualism as well and perfectly complimentary with the ideas, concepts and creations of creative explorations and lifestyle ideals. After so many years of being creative for others, jerry is redirecting his focus on two main areas, his own creative outlets and the growth of the partnership of these two soul creatives, all leading in one direction of self-expression and sharing experiences with others, something jerry has been trying to do for years. The legacy stands clear with the amount of creative variety jerry has already achieved, as well as the achievements of his partner, so the future looks amazing and expected to be one full of magic, wonder and deeply felt surprises as these two amazing souls share the life and power of creativity and nature. For jerry this journey is going back to his roots and back on the road he promised to mother nature when he was a child, over the years straying from his path by trying to fit into the social world of human existence, and listening to the advices of socially accepted mindsets, now with childhood vigour and birth dragons passion this union he has feels deeply to be the right path to take. There are many projects and adventures the two have designed and developed that will open a lot of eyes to the reality of what real living and art is all about. Let’s support them and encourage the purity of souls to shout the magical, spiritual, natural way of seeing, the way of being real.

PAINT YOUR COLORS

I see the colors of the painting. I fear the strokes of my uncertainty. I am the blacks of my decisions, and yellow and pink control the movements of my arm.

I did combine the truth of my soul with the dreams of my fantasy. I did grow the thorns of my fear. I did mix the right colors..., one day. But soon all those colored dreams faded deep into the night, with the blinding light of the rising sun.

I travel with the destiny of my actions, and I work for the rainbows of my youth. I anger at the path I’ve carved in this stone, and yet I am guided by the words around my head.

I discovered the realism when abstractions from the truth, faded under the sun, where impatience destroyed any attempt to play with my fantasy and desire. For happiness is chosen in the colors you bring, and your birth and your grave carry equal tones, one being white and the next full of all the mixers you have created.

Yet a change of heart can rework the areas of distaste; and repainting it is as easy as mixing a new pallet. Sometimes hard especially when the pallet is already muddy. But recognizing those distasteful colors is as hard as predicting the white canvas. Uncertainty and propaganda muddle your decisions.

I see the colors I have painted, I live in the destiny of my pallet, and I paint each stroke with the traditions and regret that society expects. But the painting is one of me, and to repaint is always my eyes. I live my art, and I am my painting.

Creative Artist

Jerry Verschoor
